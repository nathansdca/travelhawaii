maui

Stay at a ritzy and all-inclusive hotel, or at an eco-friendly suite hidden away in the mountains. Make sure to shop in downtown Lahaina or for the more adventurous, take a full-day drive on the Northeast side of the island full of hairpin turns and striking waterfalls. No trip is complete without watching a sunrise from Haleakala, a dormant volcano just over 10,000 feet above sea level.

Snorkel at Molokini Crater

Your trip will begin early in the morning at 7AM with a fresh fruit and bagel breakfast on the boat ride there. An experienced guide will let you know of the best spots to snorkel around. Keep your eyes open for many types of colorful fish and coral, along with occasional dolphins as well. All equipment and gear is provided.

Whale Watching Tour

A predominantly winter activity, whale watching is popular both from the shore and from a boat. Our guide will take you in a small, private boat for 4 hours while you hunt for whales. Be prepared to get wet, since it is not uncommon for humpback whales to suddenly leap out of the water in an act called breaching.

A Sunrise Surprise

This is an activity that all ages will enjoy. You must rise early in the morning, and a guide will drive you up to the summit of Haleakala so that you can watch a brilliant sunrise above the clouds. Although we provide blankets, make sure to bring extra clothing because it will be (surprisingly) cold.

Best Beaches

Maybe a relaxing day on the sand is more up your alley. Choose from Ka'anapali, Makena, or Waimea beaches, which are made up of miles upon miles of fine, white sand. If requested, we can provide all amenities, including snorkeling gear, umbrellas, and towels.
